Description

The One Health movement aims to highlight the limitless overlap between human and animal health, as well as the importance of the health of the environment they share. The One Health concept covers topics including infectious, emerging and zoonotic disease, pharmaceutical development, climate change, specie diversity, mental health, translational or comparative medicine, food safety, food security and many more topics...!

We understand that no single expertise will single-handedly be able to rise to the challenges facing global health in the coming generations. One Health is a concept that requires the input of all those interested in improving health (of humans, animals and the environment) to succeed in changing the way we think and act about health and medicine.

The medical, veterinary and scientific research communities, international development agencies, environmentalists, conservationists, engineers, policy makers and other disciplines can all bring valuable knowledge and expertise to the One Health table. It is essential that the next generation of One Health professionals are able to meet and develop these ideas together. Networking is key to ensuring the exchange, debate and development of ideas.

With this in mind, the annual Cambridge One Health Conference was started with the aim of inviting professionals from a variety of disciplines to discuss One Health topics and share their knowledge and experiences with the student community. The conference is open to anyone who is interested in the One Health concept.

Following the successes of the first two Cambridge One Health Conferences in January 2014 and January 2015, we are delighted to announce that the next conference will be held in early 2016 (date TBC) at Cambridge University, UK.

Committee Applications - Open Now

After the success of the second Cambridge One Health Conference, we are looking to recruit students for the next committee. Deadline for applications (approx 250 words): June 7th 2015. Please note that you must be a current Cambridge University student to apply for a committee role.

As a member of the Cambridge One Health Committee 2015-16, you will be involved in the organisation of the 3rd annual Cambridge University One Health conference to be held in January 2016. This provides an excellent opportunity to invite and network with professionals and students in your One Health interest area.

We are looking for enthusiastic applicants to fill the following committee roles:

  • Tech publicity officer: In charge of updating the website (HTML), email publicity to universities across the UK, posters and leaflet advertisements
  • Funding/ finance officers: We have established connections with a wide base of sponsors from previous events, including the Wellcome Trust. This role would involve contacting sponsors and submitting sponsorship requests with budget proposals, as well as following up with with sponsors after the event.
  • General logistics roles: We will allocate you a role based on previous experience or skills. This could involve roles such as contacting the university online sales teams to set up ticket sales, analysing ticket sale data, being the contact point for catering/ venue staff.

Application format:

  • Please write a statement of intent (approx. 250 words) addressing the reasons for your interest in the One Health concept and highlighting, with examples, any relevant experience or skills. Please state your course title and year of study in the application.
  • Applications will be blinded. To facilitate this process please only include your name in the document title.
  • Applications should be emailed as a word/ pdf document attachment to info@onehealthcambridge.com by June 7th 2015.

All applications will be considered, discussed, and from this, successful applicants selected by the current committee. You will be notified about the outcome of your application before the end of Easter Term. We look forward to hearing from you.
